France vs. Ukraine

2026 FIFA World Cup Qualifier • November 13, 2025

World Cup Qualification on the Line in Parisian Showdown

A pivotal 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifier is set to ignite the Parc des Princes in Paris this Thursday, November 13, 2025, as a high-flying French side hosts a determined Ukrainian team. With kick-off at 7:45 PM GMT, the stakes could not be higher. A victory for France would punch their ticket directly to the World Cup, while Ukraine is locked in a tense battle to secure a crucial playoff spot.

The Scenario: All to Play For

For France, the path is clear. Sitting comfortably at the top of Group D, three points ahead of their visitors, a win in front of their home crowd would wrap up a successful qualifying campaign with a game to spare. For Ukraine, the pressure is immense. Led by manager Serhiy Rebrov, they need a positive result to fend off a chasing Iceland and keep their World Cup dreams alive. A draw would be a valuable result, but a win would be a monumental achievement.

Team Overviews: A Clash of Styles and Stakes

France: The Star-Studded Favorites

Didier Deschamps' Les Bleus come into this fixture in formidable form, unbeaten in their last five matches. Despite their dominance, they will have to contend with a list of injuries to key players like Ousmane Dembélé, Adrien Rabiot, and Aurélien Tchouaméni.

However, the French squad boasts incredible depth. The attack will be led by the ever-dangerous Kylian Mbappé, who has been in scintillating form. The big news for the hosts is the return of midfield dynamo N'Golo Kanté. After nearly a year away from the national team, his presence is expected to provide a significant boost to their defensive stability and control of the game.

Ukraine: The Resilient Underdogs

Ukraine has proven to be a tough and resilient side throughout the qualifiers. They arrive in Paris on a three-match unbeaten run, having secured vital victories over Iceland and Azerbaijan. Their disciplined and organized approach, likely a 4-5-1 formation, will be key to frustrating the potent French attack.

The visitors are not without their own injury concerns, missing forward Artem Dovbyk. They will look to the creative spark of Ruslan Malinovskyi, who has been in fine goal-scoring form, netting three times in his last two appearances for his country. The Ukrainian spirit and work ethic will be tested, as they aim to counter-attack effectively and capitalize on any French errors.

Head-to-Head History

Previous encounters between these two nations heavily favor France. In 13 meetings, France has claimed victory seven times, with Ukraine managing just a single win. Five matches have ended in draws. The French are unbeaten in the last five clashes, including a 2-0 win in the reverse fixture earlier in this campaign.

Expert Predictions: What the Pundits Say

The consensus among football analysts is a home victory for France, though Ukraine is expected to provide a stern challenge.

  • Most experts predict a French win, with scorelines of 2-0 being a popular forecast. The return of N'Golo Kanté is seen as a major factor that will make it difficult for Ukraine to break through.
  • Some analysts anticipate a more open match, suggesting a 4-1 victory for France, noting that recent meetings have often seen both teams find the back of the net.
  • There's also a strong belief that France will assert their dominance in the second half, with some predicting the match will be a draw at halftime before France pulls away to win.

Key Betting Tips

For those interested in the markets, the analysis points towards several popular tips:

  • France to Win: The most straightforward and highly favored outcome.
  • Kylian Mbappé to Score: Given his incredible scoring record of finding the net in six of his last seven international games, he is a prime candidate to be on the scoresheet.
