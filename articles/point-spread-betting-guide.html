Mastering the Point Spread: Your Ultimate Guide to Football Betting

For anyone new to sports betting, the numbers surrounding a football game can seem like a complex code. What does +7 mean? Why does one team have a minus next to their name? You're not just picking a winner; you're betting on the margin of victory.

This is the world of point spread betting, the most popular way to wager on football. This comprehensive guide will decipher the symbols, explain the numbers, and provide the strategies you need to bet on spreads with confidence.

What is the +/- in Football Betting? The Basics of the Point Spread

The plus (+) and minus (-) signs are the foundation of spread betting. They are used to level the playing field between two teams that are not evenly matched.

  • The Favorite (-): The team expected to win is called the favorite. They will have a minus sign next to a number. This team must win the game by more than that number of points to "cover the spread."
  • The Underdog (+): The team expected to lose is the underdog. They will have a plus sign next to a number. The underdog can either win the game outright OR lose by less than that number of points to "cover the spread."

Think of it as a handicap given to the underdog. The favorite has points taken away from their final score, while the underdog gets points added.

Understanding Positive (+) Spreads: The Underdog's Advantage

When you see a plus sign, you are looking at the underdog. The number next to it is the number of points they are "getting."

What does +7 mean in football odds?

A +7 spread means the team is the underdog by a touchdown. To win a bet on a +7 team, they must either win the game or lose by 6 points or fewer. If they lose by exactly 7 points, it's a "push," and your stake is refunded.

What does +3 mean in football odds?

This indicates the team is an underdog by a field goal. To win this bet, they need to win the game or lose by 2 points or fewer. A 3-point loss would be a push.

What does a +10.5 spread mean in betting?

Your team is a significant underdog. You win the bet if they win the game or if they lose by 10 points or fewer.

What does a +14.5 spread mean?

This team is a heavy underdog of more than two touchdowns. You win if they win outright or lose by 14 points or fewer.

The Power of the Hook: Understanding Half-Points

You'll often see spreads with a half-point (.5), known as a "hook." This is crucial because it eliminates the possibility of a push.

How do you win a +3.5 spread?

Your team must win the game or lose by 3, 2, or 1 point. Since a team can't lose by 3.5 points, there can be no push.

What is a +12.5 spread?

You win your bet if the underdog team wins the game or loses by 12 or fewer points.

Understanding Negative (-) Spreads: The Favorite's Challenge

When you see a minus sign, you are looking at the favorite. They are "giving" points away.

What does (-) mean in football?

It signifies the favorite in a point spread matchup. This team is expected to win.

What is a negative 5.5 spread?

To win a bet on a -5.5 favorite, the team must win the game by 6 points or more. A victory of 1-5 points would mean your bet loses.

Is 2.5 a good spread?

Spreads around key numbers like 3 and 7 are very common. A -2.5 spread is attractive to bettors because the favorite only needs to win by a field goal (3 points) to cover.

What is a Handicap Bet?

"Handicap" is another term for point spread, often used in international markets. The meaning is identical.

  • What does handicap (-1) mean? Your team must win by 2 or more points. A 1-point win is a push.
  • What does handicap (-1.5) mean? Your team must win by 2 or more points. The half-point eliminates pushes.
  • What does handicap (-3) mean? Your team must win by 4 or more points. A 3-point win is a push.

The Price of a Bet: Why You Bet $110 to Win $100

On almost every spread bet, you will see odds of -110 listed next to the spread. This is the price of placing the bet, also known as the "juice" or "vig." This is the commission the sportsbook takes.

This built-in fee is why you risk $110 to win $100 (or $11 to win $10). It ensures the sportsbook makes money regardless of the outcome. If one person bets $110 on the favorite and another bets $110 on the underdog, the sportsbook collects $220. They pay the $210 ($100 profit + $110 stake) back to the winner and keep the $10 difference.

Strategy for Betting Football Spreads

Now that you understand the mechanics, how can you make smarter bets?

Is Point Spread Betting Risky?

All betting carries risk. Point spreads can be particularly challenging because you not only have to predict the winner but also the accuracy of the margin of victory. Upsets and unexpected game scripts are common in football, making spread betting a volatile but exciting market.

What’s the best strategy for football spreads?

There's no single "best" strategy, but successful bettors share common habits:

  • Look for Value, Not Just Winners: The goal isn't just to pick the winning team, but to find a spread that you believe is inaccurate. Do you think a -4 favorite is going to win by a touchdown? That's a value bet.
  • Understand Key Numbers: The most common margins of victory in football are 3 and 7 points. Spreads set around these numbers (e.g., -2.5, +3.5, -6.5, +7.5) are particularly important.
  • Analyze Matchups: Don't just look at team records. Dig deeper. How does a team's offensive line match up against the opponent's defensive front? Does one team have a significant special teams advantage?
  • Monitor Line Movement: Spreads move during the week for several reasons: injuries, weather reports, and a large volume of money being bet on one side. Tracking these movements can help you understand market sentiment and find value.

Do favorites usually cover the spread?

This is a common question, and the answer is no. Sportsbooks are incredibly good at setting lines. Over the long run, favorites and underdogs both cover the spread approximately 50% of the time (not accounting for pushes). This efficiency is why finding small analytical advantages is so critical.

Should beginners avoid spread betting?

Not necessarily. While more complex than a simple moneyline bet (picking the outright winner), spreads are the heart of football betting. Beginners should start with small wagers and focus on understanding why lines are set the way they are before committing significant money.

By understanding how the plus and minus signs work, what the numbers mean, and the basic strategies involved, you can move from a curious observer to an informed bettor ready to engage with the most popular way to wager on football.
